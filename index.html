Projects

Study on FPOs with analytical methods
March 2025

Under the guidance of Dr. Haricharan Behera (Indian Statistical Institute, Giridih), conducted a comprehensive evaluation of Farmers Producing Organizations (FPOs) in Jharkhand, employing advanced decision-making methodologies. Applied the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) to systematically prioritize criteria based on their relative importance and utilized the Analytic Network Process (ANP) to capture and analyze interdependencies among the criteria. Integrated both qualitative assessments (stakeholder perspectives, expert opinions) and quantitative approaches (statistical modeling, data-driven analysis) to generate actionable insights. The findings contributed to developing sustainable agricultural practices and informed policies promoting FPO efficiency and resilience.

Participatory Rural Appraisal
December 2024

Conducted participatory rural appraisal (PRA) work under the supervision of Dr. Haricharan Behera (Sociological Research Unit, Indian Statistical Institute, Giridih) focusing on the socio-economic challenges faced by individuals migrating from rural to urban areas in Jharkhand, India. The research employed PRA methods such as community mapping, focus group discussions, and seasonal calendars to explore migration patterns, drivers of migration, and the consequences of rural-to-urban transition, including challenges related to employment, housing, and social integration. By engaging both rural and urban communities, the study aimed to identify sustainable strategies for easing the transition and fostering inclusive development, contributing to the understanding of migration dynamics and alignment with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
